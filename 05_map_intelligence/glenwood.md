# MAP INTELLIGENCE — GLENWOOD
## WCS Conflict

> **Server:** WCS EU4 — Glenwood Conflict
> **Map type:** River valley city; major river N-S through center; mining district east; airport south; forested ridges
> **Orientation:** W–E axis (river N–S through center) — **spawn sides are random each match.** Either team may hold Main Base West or the eastern positions. Push priorities below are written from a west-spawn perspective; mirror them if you spawn east.
> **Doctrine basis:** OCOKA terrain analysis; ATP 3-21.8 urban and river crossing operations

## MAP OVERVIEW

Glenwood is built around a **major river running roughly N-S through the center of the map.** The city occupies both banks of the river. One team holds the western bank; the other holds the eastern bank.

**The river is the defining tactical feature.** It limits direct W–E movement to bridge crossings. Controlling the bridges controls the tempo. A team that holds all bridges and has radio coverage can fight a defensive battle from the far bank while suppressing crossings.

**FEBA:** Typically at the river crossing points — Sawmill, Gas Station, and Hospital all sit in the crossing zone. The team that holds the Gas Station supply depot maintains sustained operations in the center.

**Sector breakdown:**
- **North** (Farm → RV Park → FOB Junkyard → Radio Station → Hotel): Forest/open terrain; fewer objectives; important for relay chain
- **City center** (Rec Center → Sawmill → Gas Station → Hospital): Dense urban; CQB; crossing fights at the river
- **South** (Airport → Weed Farm → Radar Base): Open ground; airport complex; east team's southern anchor
- **East** (Lower Mines → Upper Mines → Power Station → North Tower): East team's primary territory; elevated terrain; mining industry

**High-value observation post positions:**
- Hotel rooftop — covers the river crossings and northern approach road
- FOB Overlook elevated position — covers western approach and Sawmill crossing
- Upper Mines high ground — covers lower mines, eastern road, and center approaches
- North Tower — observation of the entire northern sector from the east team's position

## SUPPLY ROUTES

**West spawn logistics:**
- Main Base West → FOB Overlook → Gas Station (primary W supply corridor into city)
- Main Base West → FOB Junkyard → Radio Station (northern supply route)
- FOB Junkyard → Rec Center → Sawmill (city center supply via northern route)

**East spawn logistics:**
- Power Station → North Tower → Hotel (northern eastern approach)
- Lower Mines → Airport → Radar Base (southern eastern corridor)
- Upper Mines → Hospital (eastern approach to center)

**Supply interdiction priority:** The road from FOB Overlook to Gas Station is the west team's primary supply line into the city. Ambushing this route cuts their ability to maintain the forward supply depot. East team analog: the Power Station → Hotel road is the primary eastern logistics route — interdicting here isolates the east team's northern presence.

**River crossing under fire:** Supply vehicles attempting to cross the river at Sawmill or Hospital are extremely vulnerable. Any vehicle crossing should be escorted and preceded by infantry suppression of the far bank. A technical with an MG or vehicle-mounted weapons covering the far bank is the minimum crossing support.

---

## STRATEGIC PRIORITY

**West spawn push priority:**
1. **Rec Center / Gas Station** — secure the city center and establish the forward supply depot at Gas Station
2. **Radio Station / Hotel** — northern relay chain; Hotel observation dominates the crossing fight
3. **FOB Overlook** — secures the western approach; named FOB likely has pre-built defenses
4. **Sawmill** — river crossing fight; necessary to push to eastern bank
5. **Hospital** — southern city push; sets condition for Airport and eastern mines approach

**River crossing decision:** Do not attempt to cross the river at multiple points simultaneously without communication. Crossing forces are vulnerable — one element crosses while another suppresses the far bank. Never cross a bridge without first clearing the far bank with fire.

**Mines priority:** The Upper Mines elevation is worth capturing early if the east team is not yet entrenched — it controls the entire eastern sector. Once the east team fortifies it, it requires a direct assault with MG suppression.

---

## RADIO RELAY PRIORITIES

- **Gas Station** is likely the central relay hub for the city — capture it and you unlock the surrounding objectives
- **Radio Station** in the north is explicitly named; it extends coverage to the northern chain (Hotel, FOB Junkyard, RV Park)
- **Radar Base** in the SW likely extends coverage to Airport and Weed Farm southern chain
- Mobile relay vehicle run along the western bank (west team-held) can extend coverage across the river before infantry crosses

---

## MORTAR COVERAGE NOTES

- River crossing points (Sawmill bridge, Hospital crossing) are the highest-value preplanned targets on the map — any enemy that stacks at the crossing while you're on the far bank dies to one mortar mission
- Airport open ground is an ideal beaten zone — full suppression effect; pre-register TRP on the runway approach
- Upper Mines complex can be ranged from the city center position once an OP is established at Hotel or FOB Overlook
