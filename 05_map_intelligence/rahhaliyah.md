# MAP INTELLIGENCE — RAHHALIYAH
## WCS Conflict

> **Server:** WCS NA9 — Rahhaliyah Conflict
> **Map type:** Desert/arid Middle Eastern terrain; open sightlines; coastal port NE; airfield south; flat with low urban scatter
> **Orientation:** N–S axis — **spawn sides are random each match.** Either team may hold Main Base North or the southern positions. Push priorities below are written from a north-spawn perspective; mirror them if you spawn south.
> **Doctrine basis:** OCOKA terrain analysis; ATP 3-21.8 desert and open terrain operations

## MAP OVERVIEW

Rahhaliyah is a **desert map with the longest engagement ranges of any WCS map.** Open terrain dominates the majority of the map — cover is scarce, distances are large, and vehicles are far more lethal here than on any other server.

**Axis of advance:** The main N-S axis runs from Main Base North through Zab → Northern Industrial Zone → Northern Compound → Insurgent Compound → Airfield. A secondary W axis diverges through Western Market District and Afar.

**FEBA:** Typically establishes at the Northern Compound / Insurgent Compound line in the center. The flat terrain means whoever controls elevation (any low ridge or compound wall) has a massive spotting advantage.

**Critical difference from other WCS maps:** There is almost no concealment outside of buildings and compound walls. Tactical movement requires bounding overwatch between buildings rather than through vegetation. Vehicle dominance is at maximum here — unprotected infantry crossing open ground is almost always fatal.

| **Western Market District buildings** | Center-W | Only cover on the western approach; buildings provide the sole concealment on that axis |
| **Rahhaliyah Airfield structures** | S | Terminal and hangar buildings are the only cover on the southern objective; everything else is runway |
| **Low ridges / berms** | Scattered center | Any terrain 2m above grade gives a decisive spotting and suppression advantage |

**High-value observation post positions:**
- Rahhaliyah Port elevated structure — covers Farm Fields approach and eastern axis; LRF range exceeds 1000m in clear desert terrain
- Any compound rooftop in Northern Compound area — 360-degree observation in open desert
- Industrial Zone rooftop — covers the northern approach road to Zab and Main Base North

## SUPPLY ROUTES

The road network (shown in brown on Rahhaliyah) is sparser than other maps. Many moves are cross-country.

**North spawn logistics:**
- Main Base North → Zab → Northern Industrial Zone (primary N supply lane)
- Northern Industrial Zone → Northern Compound → Insurgent Compound (main push corridor)
- Alt route: Main Base North → Rahhaliyah Port → Farm Fields (eastern flanking supply)

**South spawn logistics:**
- Airfield → Militia Outpost → Insurgent Compound (primary S–N supply push)
- Hillah → Insurgent Compound (eastern approach)
- Afar → Western Market District (western supply)

**Supply interdiction priority:** The road between Northern Industrial Zone and Northern Compound is the primary north team supply choke — cutting it strands north team forward elements without resupply. An AT team and an MG on the eastern side of the road ambush zone can seal this route completely; there is nowhere to flank off-road.

**Vehicle ambush geometry:** L-shaped ambush on the main road is extremely effective — the open terrain means there is nowhere for the convoy to go once the kill zone is established. First and last vehicle sequence (trail first, then lead) works perfectly on this map's road corridors.

## STRATEGIC PRIORITY

**North spawn push priority:**
1. **Zab + Northern Industrial Zone** — first push establishes relay chain and a FOB in north team forward territory
2. **Rahhaliyah Port** — eastern flanking option; if the south team leaves it open, it provides a flanking approach to Farm Fields
3. **Northern Compound** — mid-map anchor; radio coverage toward Insurgent Compound and both flanks
4. **Farm Fields** (★ primary) — high victory point value; contest and hold to drain enemy reserves
5. **Insurgent Compound** — center choke; holding this with Northern Compound effectively seals the map

**The all-arms requirement:** This map is where combined arms is mandatory, not optional. Infantry crossing open ground without vehicle fire support will be eliminated. Every push requires:
- Vehicle (technical or IFV) providing overwatch and suppression
- Infantry bounding between hard cover (buildings and compound walls only)
- Recon/sniper at max range calling vehicle movements before they engage

**Eastern flank (Rahhaliyah Port → Farm Fields):** If the south team is not patrolling the eastern road, this is a fast north-spawn route to the primary (★) objective. Check the Port first — if it is empty or lightly held, a fast assault through there can seize Farm Fields before the south team responds.

## RADIO RELAY PRIORITIES

- **Northern Industrial Zone** is likely the first major relay node — its named FOB status suggests pre-built relay capability
- **Northern Compound** is the center hub — once captured, it opens both Insurgent Compound and the flanking chains
- **Farm Fields** at the center-east may be offset from the chain — verify relay connection before committing a holding force

## MORTAR COVERAGE NOTES

- **Most effective map for area suppression mortar fire** — no concealment means beaten zones take full effect
- Farm Fields approach is the single highest-value preplanned target: open ground, converging approach vectors, primary objective traffic
- Airfield runways are a massive beaten zone opportunity — any south team reinforcement column staging at the airfield can be ranged from a mortar positioned north of Insurgent Compound
- Zab approach from the south: pre-register a TRP on the road south of Zab; the south team will use it for every counter-push
