# MAP INTELLIGENCE — ROSTOV
## WCS Conflict / AAS

> **Server:** WCS NA — Rostov Conflict / Rostov AAS
> **Map type:** Semi-urban, mixed forest, river lake complex in NW
> **Orientation:** N–S axis — **spawn sides are random each match.** Either team may hold Main Base North or the southern positions. Push priorities below are written from a north-spawn perspective; mirror them if you spawn south.
> **Doctrine basis:** OCOKA terrain analysis; ATP 3-21.8 area of operations planning

---

> **⚠️ CODENAME SYSTEM — READ FIRST**
>
> The magenta names printed next to each objective (e.g., KANSAS, NEVADA) are **randomly assigned radio codewords that rotate every match.** They are NOT fixed to locations. The objective at Junkyard might be NEVADA in one session and COLORADO in the next.
>
> **How to use this document:** Memorize the fixed location names (Junkyard, Powerplant, etc.) and their positions relative to each other. When you spawn into a session, open the map and cross-reference the current codeword to the location name. All comms during that session use the codeword — never the location name.
>
> **Example session rotation (from one captured screenshot):**
> KANSAS = Main Base North | NEVADA = Junkyard | WYOMING = Powerplant | BOSTON = Eastern Checkpoint | NEW YORK = Chernihiv | FLORIDA = Overgrown Radio | PROVIDENCE = Relay | AMBER = Airfield | OMAHA = Sawmill | KENTUCKY = Atamano | IOWA = Southern Farm

## MAP OVERVIEW

Rostov is a **medium-sized map with a roughly N-S oriented main axis of advance.** One team's main base anchors the northern edge; the other pushes from the south. The western portion of the map features water/lake terrain that channels movement. The road network runs diagonally NW-SE and N-S.

**FEBA (Forward Edge of Battle Area):** Typically establishes at the Chernihiv / Eastern Checkpoint / Sawmill line across the center of the map. The first team to anchor this line with radio relay coverage controls the chokepoint.

**Engagement distances:** Medium to long across open fields (300–600m). Short in Sawmill, Chernihiv, and Airfield built-up areas (50–150m).

## KEY TERRAIN (OCOKA)

| Feature | Location | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Elevated ridge** | NE (above Junkyard/Powerplant) | Dominates eastern axis; MG and sniper position; LRF range controls road below |
| **River/lake complex** | NW | Hard barrier west of Relay; channelizes west-bank movement through limited crossing points |
| **Chernihiv road network** | Center | Yellow road convergence; vehicle movement forced through this node |
| **Airfield open ground** | SW | No cover for 200m+ in multiple directions; fatal to approach in the open |
| **Sawmill structure** | Center-SW | Interior cover; dominant structure visible from multiple approaches |

**High-value observation post positions:**
- Ridge NE of Junkyard — covers eastern axis, Powerplant, and Eastern Checkpoint simultaneously
- Treeline north of Chernihiv — concealed overwatch of the center road

## SUPPLY ROUTES

Primary supply movement follows the **yellow road network** visible on the map. Key routes:

**North spawn logistics:**
- Main Base North → Junkyard → Eastward to Powerplant / Checkpoint (northern supply corridor)
- Main Base North → Relay → Airfield (western supply corridor)
- Cross-center: Chernihiv to Overgrown Radio (central lateral road)

**South spawn logistics:**
- Southern Farm → Atamano → Sawmill → Chernihiv (western push lane)
- Eastern approach: Up the eastern road toward Checkpoint and Powerplant

**Supply interdiction priority:** The Sawmill–Chernihiv road segment is the highest-value interdiction target. Cutting this route isolates the south team's resupply to the central objectives from both main approaches.

## STRATEGIC PRIORITY

Objectives are connected by radio relay chains. Capture order matters — you cannot score an objective outside your radio network.

**North spawn push priority:**
1. **Junkyard** — establishes your first contested point and expands radio coverage east
2. **Chernihiv** — center anchor; sets conditions for two-axis push
3. **Eastern Checkpoint + Powerplant** — secures eastern flank; closes the south team's path to the center
4. **Relay** — opens western axis to Airfield and Sawmill
5. **Sawmill** — breaks the south team's center-west anchor

**Axis selection:** The eastern axis (Junkyard → Powerplant → Checkpoint) tends to be faster with vehicle support but is exposed to long-range fire from the ridge. The western axis (Relay → Airfield → Sawmill) is covered terrain but requires crossing the water obstacle.

**Do not push to Atamano or Southern Farm** until the center is secured — they are behind the south team's main LOC and will be reinforced before you can capitalize.

---

## RADIO RELAY PRIORITIES

Radio relay extends your team's capture network. Relay nodes that are worth an early combat engineer/logistics run:
- **Relay (objective)** — its capture extends radio to the western objectives (Airfield, Sawmill) in one move
- **Overgrown Radio** — provides eastern flank coverage; completes the eastern chain to Checkpoint

Do not advance past Chernihiv on either axis without first confirming relay coverage. Check the objective chain lines on the map before pushing.

## MORTAR COVERAGE NOTES

- Junkyard and Powerplant are within range of a well-placed mortar position in the NE treelines (~700–900m from Main Base North direction)
- Chernihiv center can be ranged from elevated terrain to the north
- Sawmill is in dead ground from many northern positions — requires forward OP placement to observe and adjust
