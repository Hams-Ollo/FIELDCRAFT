## WCS Conflict

> **Server:** WCS NA / WCS EU — NATO/RUS Chernarus Conflict

## MAP OVERVIEW

Chernarus is WCS's **largest map** — a sprawling NE–SW oriented terrain with two major push corridors running in parallel:

1. **Eastern coastal corridor** — Runs N–S along the entire east shore: Industrial → Solnichniy → Coastal Base → Elektrozavodsk. Mostly open ground near the coast with excellent vehicle lanes but extended exposure to long-range fire from the inland ridgeline.

2. **Interior crossroads route** — Runs through the forested hills: Gorka → Novy Sobor → Mogilevka → Chernogorsk. Rolling terrain with tree cover; better for dismounted movement but slower vehicle routes.

**FEBA (Forward Edge of Battle Area):** Typically stabilizes at the Mogilevka / Staroye / Industrial line running roughly east-west across the center of the map. The first team to anchor all three simultaneously with relay coverage holds the map's decisive terrain.

**Third axis — Western flank:** Pogorevka → Zelenogorsk → Kozlovka routes are a slow, covered flanking option. Rarely decisive alone but forces the opposing team to split forces if left uncontested.

**Engagement distances:** Far longer than other WCS maps. Open-ground engagements routinely reach 400–700m on the coastal corridor and in the northern approaches to Gorka. CQB zones: Chernogorsk (tight urban blocks), Novy Sobor (village), Elektrozavodsk (industrial structures). Expect mixed ranges — plan loadout accordingly.

## SUPPLY ROUTES

Primary supply movement follows the **yellow road network.** Chernarus has two main arteries and one flanking route.

**Northeast spawn logistics:**
- Main Base NE → Gorka → Novy Sobor (interior central axis — primary supply corridor)
- Main Base NE → Industrial → Solnichniy → Coastal Base (eastern coastal corridor — vehicle-heavy route)
- Lateral: Novy Sobor ↔ Staroye ↔ Industrial (cross-map connector — keeps both axes supplied)

**Southwest spawn logistics:**
- Komarovo / Chernogorsk → Elektrozavodsk → Coastal Base (south coast road — primary)
- Chernogorsk → Mogilevka → Novy Sobor (interior push axis)
- Kozlovka → Pogorevka → Zelenogorsk (western flank route — slow, but covered)

**Supply interdiction priority:** The **Novy Sobor crossroads** is the single highest-value interdiction target on the map. Cutting or mining this junction forces the NE team to choose one corridor and abandons the other — splitting attention and reducing resupply rate by roughly half. **The Mogilevka–Chernogorsk road segment** is the equivelant chokepoint for south-push logistics.

## STRATEGIC PRIORITY

**Northeast spawn push priority:**
1. **Gorka** — secures first forward point; extends radio southeast toward Novy Sobor and east toward Industrial
2. **Novy Sobor** — critical interior crossroads; opens both the interior corridor and the connection to the eastern axis
3. **Staroye** — center anchor; connects east and interior; prevents south team from splitting the map
4. **Industrial** — easternmost contested point on the north push; secures the coastal corridor's northern entry
5. **Mogilevka** — FEBA anchor on the interior route; sets conditions for the final south coast push
6. **Coastal Base / Solnichniy** — close the east corridor; cuts south team's coastal resupply
7. **Chernogorsk / Elektrozavodsk** — terminal objectives; urban combat — allocate additional infantry

**Axis selection:** Start with the interior route (Gorka → Novy Sobor → Mogilevka) to secure the crossroads before committing vehicles to the exposed coastal corridor. Once Novy Sobor is held, push Industrial simultaneously on the eastern axis. Never push Chernogorsk until Mogilevka is locked — an unsecured center will let the south team roll your interior while you're fighting in the city.

**Western flank:** Pogorevka is a useful flanking objective that forces the opposing team to peel forces away from center. If Pogorevka falls early, push to Zelenogorsk — this threatens to encircle any south-team defense of Mogilevka. Do not deep-push Kozlovka until the center is won.

## RADIO RELAY PRIORITIES

Chernarus has the largest relay chain of any WCS map. Radio coverage is harder to maintain across the full AO. Priority relay investments:

- **Novy Sobor** — central relay node; its capture alone extends coverage to Pogorevka, Gorka, and Staroye in most configurations
- **Staroye** — links the interior chain to the eastern coastal chain; critical for maintaining dual-axis coverage
- **Green Mountain structure** (if a relay exists here) — western coverage relay; covers Pogorevka and Zelenogorsk

Do not push to Industrial or Solnichniy without confirming relay chain is intact through Novy Sobor → Staroye. Long-range gaps in relay will drop objective capture credit even after clearing.
