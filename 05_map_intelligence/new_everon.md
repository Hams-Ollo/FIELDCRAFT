# MAP INTELLIGENCE — NEW EVERON
## WCS Conflict / AAS

> **Server:** WCS NA3 — New Everon Conflict
> **Map type:** Island map; mountainous south, open farmland center, peninsula north
> **Orientation:** N–S axis — **spawn sides are random each match.** Either team may hold Main Base North (peninsula) or the southern bases across the island. Push priorities below are written from a north-spawn perspective; mirror them if you spawn south.
> **Doctrine basis:** OCOKA terrain analysis; ATP 3-21.8 area of operations planning

---

> **⚠️ CODENAME SYSTEM — READ FIRST**
>
> The magenta names printed next to each objective are **randomly assigned radio codewords that rotate every match.** They are NOT fixed to locations. The objective at Military Hospital might be DOLG today and ARGON tomorrow.
>
> **How to use this document:** Memorize the fixed location names (Saint-Phillippe, Military Hospital, etc.) and their geographic positions. When you spawn, open the map and match the current session's codewords to these locations. All comms use the codeword — never the location name.
>

## MAP OVERVIEW

New Everon is the **largest and most complex WCS map.** It is a full island with a narrow peninsula projecting north, a mountainous southern half, and relatively open farmland in the center.

**Axis of advance:** The main N-S axis runs from Main Base North down the peninsula spine through Saint-Phillippe → Montignac → Figari to the southern team's positions. A secondary W axis runs through Lamentin.

**FEBA:** Typically establishes at the Montignac / Military Hospital line across the center of the island. The peninsula is a natural choke — the team that holds the junction between peninsula and main island controls the early game.

**Map scale note:** New Everon has significantly more empty terrain between objectives than Serhiivka or Glenwood. Movement times are longer. This rewards recon, relay positioning, and vehicle use more than other maps.

## KEY TERRAIN (OCOKA)

| Feature | Location | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Peninsula spine (N)** | N peninsula | Linear terrain that compresses the N axis into a single corridor; ambush and choke opportunity |
| **Peninsula junction** | Where peninsula meets main island | Control here separates the north main base from the main island; critical early defense/attack point |
| **Central ridge/elevation** | W of Montignac | High ground that dominates western approaches; OP position for the entire center |
| **Saint-Phillippe town** | N/center | Largest infrastructure feature in the north; road hub; radio relay coverage of N half |
| **Coastal terrain (W)** | Lamentin area | Coastline restricts western approach; routes reduced to road chokepoints |
| **Southern mountain mass** | S half | Dense terrain that slows vehicle movement and creates natural defensive lines |

**High-value observation post positions:**
- Ridgeline west of Montignac — covers both approaches to the center
- Northern peninsula high point — overlooks the entire peninsula corridor
- Farm area elevated ground — open-ground observation of center-east approaches

## SUPPLY ROUTES

**North spawn logistics:**
- Main Base North → down the peninsula road → Saint-Phillippe → Serret (primary N supply lane)
- Saint-Phillippe → Montignac (main island supply extension)
- Montignac → Lamentin (western axis supply)

**South spawn logistics:**
- Base Levie → Figari → Montignac (primary S–N push route)
- Lamentin → W coastal road → Montignac (western flanking supply)
- Scattered eastern routes through farm terrain

**Supply interdiction priority:** The Serret–Saint-Phillippe road is the primary choke for the north team's supply moving south. An ambush here cuts the main base connection to the fight. The Figari–Montignac road is the equivalent south team vulnerability.

**Vehicle transit warning:** The peninsula road between Main Base North and Saint-Phillippe is a linear kill zone. A single AT team or vehicle can seal the north team's main supply route. Always have a security element clearing the peninsula road before running logistics convoys.

---

## STRATEGIC PRIORITY

**North spawn push priority:**
1. **Serret / Rassvet area** — hold the peninsula junction; prevents the south team from pushing the choke northward
2. **Saint-Phillippe** — N/center anchor; radio coverage for all northern objectives
3. **Military Hospital** — advances the FEBA to the N/center line
4. **Montignac** — center anchor; radio coverage for both eastern and western branches
5. **Lamentin** — secures W axis; denies the south team the coastal flanking route

**AAS mode note:** In AAS (Advance and Secure), objectives open sequentially — the next objective only becomes capturable once the current one is secured. On New Everon AAS, the attack sequence is fixed. Coordinate team-wide commitment to the current objective rather than spreading across the map. The team that masses on the AAS objective while the enemy defends wins the tempo battle.

**Do not split force east and west until Montignac is secured** — it is the central relay hub, and trying to hold objectives on both flanks without the center is unsustainable.

---

## RADIO RELAY PRIORITIES

- **Saint-Phillippe** is the primary northern relay hub — its capture opens coverage to the Rassvet cluster and the peninsula sub-points
- **Montignac** connects north and south halves — it is the most critical relay node on the map
- **Lamentin** extends coverage to the western coast chain
- A solo logistics player running a mobile command vehicle (if available) can leapfrog relay to unlinked objectives — high-value task

---

## MORTAR COVERAGE NOTES

- The peninsula corridor is a natural mortar kill zone — pre-registered TRP on the road between Serret and Rassvet pays dividends when the enemy stacks the choke
- Montignac center road intersection can be pre-registered; it receives high traffic from both factions
- Farm area (open ground) is ideal beaten-zone ground — full suppression effect on any movement
