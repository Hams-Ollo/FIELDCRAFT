# SNIPER / RECON ROLE GUIDE
## Long-Range Observation and Precision Engagement for WCS Conflict

> **Doctrine basis:** ATP 3-21.8 (Sniper Operations); FM 7-93 (Long Range Surveillance)
> **Application:** WCS servers — recon, long-range fire support, HVT interdiction
> **Sources:** King Alex Gameplay Guide; King Alex TacTalk Sniper/Spotter Episode

---

## THE SNIPER MINDSET

> *"Ninety percent of what a sniper does is observe and report. Ten percent is shooting."*

The sniper is not primarily a killer. The sniper is an intelligence collection platform that can also destroy high-value targets on demand. Observation, position selection, route planning, reporting, and fire coordination are the majority of the job.

A sniper who moves well, observes accurately, reports consistently, and calls effective fire missions destroys more enemy combat power than a player with fifty kills.

A sniper who moves carelessly, engages everything they see, and burns their position for a single kill destroys one player and then gets killed — a net neutral at best.

**Rule 1: Survive. Rule 2: Observe. Rule 3: Engage when it matters.**

## SNIPER / SPOTTER PAIR STRUCTURE

The sniper and spotter operate as a two-person team. This is not optional.

**Spotter — Higher rank, team in charge:**
- Responsible for planning the overall route and mission
- Carries all thermal and spotting optics, additional equipment (smoke, AP mines, AT satchels)
- Reads the rangefinder; calls range, bearing, and mortar adjustments
- Makes the go/no-go call on engagements
- Operates the radio — maintains communication with command and mortar crews

**Sniper — Selects the micro-route:**
- Carries the precision rifle + pistol + minimal additional load
- Selects the exact path between waypoints given by the spotter
- Responsible for the shot itself
- The rank hierarchy relaxes once in position — both members are equal in the hide

> **WCS note:** When running as a solo sniper/recon player, prioritize the spotter role's responsibilities: radio, optics, and planning over pure shooting. A lone shooter who cannot report is a waste of a sniper slot.

> **Bolt-action precision rifle:** Not in WCS at time of recording — anticipated future addition. Current long-range work uses DMR platforms. All principles below apply.

## LOADOUTS

**Sniper:**
- Primary: Individual weapon (standard rifle for CQB contingency)
- Long-range: Precision rifle / DMR with highest available magnification optic
- Sidearm: Pistol
- Minimal additional kit — be light enough to move and disappear

**Spotter:**
- Primary: DMR or standard rifle
- Optics: All thermal, all spotting scopes, Laser Rangefinder (mandatory)
- Equipment: Smoke (marking), AP mines (position protection), AT satchels (vehicle interdiction when opportunity presents)
- The spotter carries the team's support equipment; heavier kit is acceptable

## RANGEFINDER TECHNIQUES

Laser Rangefinder advanced functions — mastery of these separates effective recon from basic spotting.

| Input | Output |
|---|---|
| **Hold `R`** | Range to a single point |
| **Hold `V`** | Bearing to a single point |
| **Hold `R` → tap `C` → release at second point** | Distance between two points |
| **Hold `V` → tap `C` → release at second point** | Direction AND distance between two points |
| **Hold `V` → tap `C` (mortar mode) → mark first point → drag to second** | Mortar Left/Up adjustment numbers for the gun crew |

**Mortar adjustment mode (V + tap C):**
Mark the current round's impact with first point, then drag to where you want the next round to land. The rangefinder outputs the exact left/up adjustment to pass to the mortar operator. This eliminates math from the observer side entirely — just read the numbers, pass them to the crew.

## FINAL FIRING POSITION (FFP) SELECTION

Every position you commit to must satisfy all five criteria:

1. **Observation** — unobstructed line of sight to the target area
2. **Concealment from objective** — the enemy cannot observe your position from the target area
3. **Backdrop** — position against a dark or textured background; never silhouetted against sky
4. **Arcs of fire** — engagement arcs cover the target without exposing you to counter-observation
5. **Multiple egress routes** — at least two ways out if compromised

**The wood line rule:** Never observe from the edge of a treeline. Edge observers are silhouetted and visible. Move **15–20 meters back into the wood line** and observe from depth. You can see out; they cannot see in.

**Plan three positions before departing:** Primary, secondary, and tertiary. If your primary has poor LOS or is compromised, move to secondary without returning to base. Never commit to a single option.

## ROUTE PLANNING — PWCAMDRASS

British Army route-planning mnemonic — applied here as principle-based guidance for WCS terrain navigation.

| Letter | Factor | Application |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | Plowed fields | Soft plowed earth bogs movement; go around |
| **W** | Wildlife | Unnatural animal behavior alerts the enemy. Cows investigate unusual movement. Sheep flee threats. Wildlife is an early warning system — use it and respect it. |
| **C** | Crossings | Cross roads at bends where sight lines are shortest. Minimize exposure at linear danger areas. |
| **A** | Avoid steep slopes / skylining | Ridgelines silhouette you; steep slopes exhaust you. Traverse the contour — move across the side of the hill, not over the top. |
| **M** | Maintain backdrop | Always have something behind you when moving. Open sky = silhouette = target. |
| **D** | stop-Look-listen pauses | Movement creates noise and visual disturbance. Stop periodically, observe, listen, then continue. |
| **R** | Roads | Do not walk roads. Roads channel patrols, vehicles, and observation. Cross quickly; do not travel along them. |
| **A** | Avoid extensions | Avoid ridge extensions, spurs, and promontories visible from multiple directions. |
| **SS** | Stop points / Check behind | Pre-plan observation halts at likely danger areas. Always check your back trail periodically. |

**Core principle:** Traverse contour lines, not ridgelines. The side of a hill gives you a backdrop while keeping you below the skyline.

## EFFECTIVE ENGAGEMENT RANGES

| Range | Realistic Expectation |
|---|---|
| ~400m | Standard combat engagement; headshot achievable with practice |
| ~900m | First-round hit on a stationary target — excellent |
| ~1400m | Harassment and suppression; enemy cannot effectively return fire at this range |

Do not engage beyond your effective range. It wastes ammunition, reveals your position, and gives the enemy a direction of fire without achieving a hit.

## TARGET PRIORITY LIST

Engage in this order. Do not waste a shot on a low-value target while a high-value target is available.

| Priority | Target | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — IMMEDIATE** | Enemy snipers | An active enemy sniper cancels your mission and hunts your teammates. Engage immediately, over everything else. |
| 2 | Officers and NCOs | Disrupts command and control. Leaderless units are slower and more predictable. |
| 3 | Signalers and radio operators | Destroys the enemy's ability to call fire missions, request reinforcement, and coordinate maneuver. |
| 4 | Crew-served weapons | MG teams, AT teams, mortar crews. Eliminates precision fire support. |
| 5 | Equipment | Vehicles, supply trucks, technical assets. |
| 6 | Optics | Thermal scopes, spotting equipment. Degrades enemy intelligence collection. |
| 7 | Grounded aircraft | Engage before it lifts, not after. |

> **Never engage indiscriminately.** One shot on the enemy Commander is worth more than ten shots on random riflemen.

## REPORTING — SALUTE AND TAX

**SALUTE — Standard spot report for infantry and general enemy forces:**

| Letter | Element | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Size | "Squad-sized element, ~8 personnel" |
| **A** | Activity | "Establishing fighting positions, digging in" |
| **L** | Location | "Grid 085 042, south treeline" |
| **U** | Uniform | "Russian woodland camo, carrying AKs" |
| **T** | Time | "Time now" or specific time |
| **E** | Equipment | "One PKM, two RPG-7s" |

**TAX — Weapon and crew-served equipment report:**

| Letter | Element | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **T** | Type | What is the weapon system |
| **A** | Ammo | What ammunition type it is carrying or firing |
| **X** | Carriage | How it is mounted or transported (vehicle-mounted, crew-served static, man-packed) |
| **S** | Sights | What optics or capabilities it has |

> **Position security:** Never mark your own confirmed position on a permanent map. Use a clear overlay layer that can be discarded if you are compromised.

## SYNCHRONIZED SNIPER + INDIRECT FIRE

The most effective sniper mission combines a precision shot with immediate indirect fire chaos.

**Procedure:**
1. Identify your High Value Target (HVT) — typically an officer or NCO commanding the position.
2. **Pre-brief the mortar crew first.** Transmit the full fire mission as *"at my command"*. The mortar is loaded and oriented — the crew is waiting for your trigger word.
3. Observe and confirm the HVT. When ready, take the shot.
4. **Immediately call "Fire"** over the net as the target drops.

**Why this works:** The window between an HVT going down and the enemy adapting is measured in seconds. Pre-briefing the mortar crew compresses the gap between your shot and the first round on target to near-zero. Enemy starts reacting to their commander's death and the mortars hit before they can rally, take cover, or communicate. The combined effect is catastrophically disproportionate to either action alone.

## MULTI-ANGLE OBSERVER CORRECTION

When you are positioned at a significant angle to the mortar-to-target axis, your "left" and "right" do not match the mortar crew's "left" and "right."

**The problem:** You are 90 degrees to the side of the gun-target line. You call "right 50." The crew adjusts their weapon right 50 — but in their frame of reference, that is actually *toward* you or *away* from the target, not the direction you intended.

**Solutions:**
1. Use the rangefinder's mortar adjustment mode (V + tap C) — it outputs corrections in the gun crew's reference frame automatically
2. Give corrections in mils rather than left/right — mils are absolute, not relative to observer angle
3. Mark the impact and target on the map and let the crew calculate their own correction from their position

> **When in doubt, use the rangefinder mortar mode.** It handles the angle translation for you.

## HIT AND RUN vs SUSTAINED ENGAGEMENT

**Hit and run (default):** Strike and immediately relocate. Clearance patrols launch within seconds of a sniper engagement. Move at least 200 meters in a new direction before re-establishing any position.

**Sustained engagement (when the math justifies it):**
- When you can drain a defensive position faster than it reinforces (suppressing a key crew-served weapon while ground forces assault)
- When you are actively protecting ground forces in contact and your position is screened
- When the target is time-sensitive enough to accept the counter-sniper risk

Default is hit and run. Sustained is the exception.

## COLORED SMOKE USAGE

| Color | Standard Use |
|---|---|
| **Yellow** | LZ (Landing Zone) marking for aircraft |
| **Green** | Enemy position reference for CAS or indirect fire |
| **Red** | Enemy position (alternate; varies by server convention) |
| **White** | Deception and withdrawal concealment |

Never use visible smoke to mark your own position during active enemy contact.

---

## ENEMY RADIO LOOTING

When a high-value enemy target is neutralized, check the body for a radio. **Picking up an enemy radio gives you access to enemy comms.** You can listen to the enemy net for:
- Reinforcement plans and timings
- Movement orders ("push to Grid 063")
- Medical calls that reveal how hard you hurt them
- Command-level decisions about which objective to prioritize

This is live intelligence that cannot be obtained any other way. Brief your Commander immediately with anything actionable.
