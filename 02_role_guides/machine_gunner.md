# MACHINE GUNNER ROLE GUIDE
## Crew-Served Automatic Weapons for WCS Conflict

> **Application:** WCS servers — MG team operations, rates of fire, crew coordination
> **Source:** Machine Gun Team Training
> **Note:** Source material is Arma 3 — AG mechanics (ACE system) and belt-linking specifics may differ in Reforger. Core principles — rates of fire, crew roles, talking guns — are universal and translate directly.

---

## THE MG TEAM

The machine gun is the squad's principal casualty-producing weapon. Everything else in the squad exists to support it getting into position and keeping it fed. The MG's primary purpose is to pin enemy forces with suppressive fire so the assault element can maneuver.

An MG team is a **two-person crew minimum:**
- **Gunner** — operates the weapon, controls rates of fire, maintains position
- **Assistant Gunner (AG)** — manages ammunition, links fresh belts, provides close-in security

The AG stands at the **gunner's left side**. This is the position from which the AG can monitor the feed tray, link incoming belts, and observe the ammo count without crossing in front of the muzzle.

---

## RATES OF FIRE

Three rates, each with a specific purpose and a specific ammunition cost. Know which to use and when to transition.

### Mad Minute — Cyclic Fire

**Duration:** 10 seconds of continuous fire
**Purpose:** Shock action. Ambush initiation. Opening of a squad assault.
**Effect:** Maximum psychological and physical impact in the opening seconds of contact.
**Cost:** High ammunition expenditure. Cannot be sustained.

**When to use:** Ambush trigger — hold until the vehicle is fully inside the kill zone, then open with the mad minute. Or on a final assault when you need maximum suppression for the element crossing the final 50 meters to the objective.

**Count:** No counting. Work the trigger for 10 seconds, then transition immediately to rapid.

---

### Rapid Fire

**Duration:** 10–13 round burst, fired every 2 seconds
**Mnemonic:** *"Die [pause] die [pause] die"* — roughly the cadence of a 10–13 round burst
**Pause:** Count 1,000 → 2,000 → fire again

**When to use:** Immediately after the mad minute while the initial shock effect is still on the enemy. Maintain rapid for 20–30 seconds, then drop to sustained for the long engagement.

---

### Sustained Fire

**Duration:** 6–9 round burst, fired every 4 seconds
**Count:** 1,000 → 2,000 → 3,000 → 4,000 → fire

**Critical rule: Count out loud.** Not to yourself — out loud. The count forces rhythm discipline. Rushing the count burns through ammo ahead of the plan and destroys your engagement timeline exactly when the assault element is depending on you most.

**When to use:** Any prolonged engagement. Support by fire while the assault element maneuvers. Holding a defensive position. Any mission where the gun must maintain fire presence over several minutes.

> **Do not rush sustained fire.** The purpose of sustained rate is to extend the engagement window to match the assault element's timeline. A gunner who burns ammo at rapid instead of transitioning to sustained wastes the asset — and potentially strands the assault element without base of fire at the worst possible moment.

## PRE-MISSION AMMO CALCULUS

Before every mission, calculate your sustained fire budget:

1. Count the total rounds across all belts
2. Sustained rate produces approximately **90–135 rounds per minute** at standard cadence
3. Divide total rounds by 90–135 = your **minimum minutes of sustained fire**
4. Brief the squad leader and Commander: *"We can give you X minutes of sustained base of fire at sustained rate before we go dry."*

This lets command plan the assault timeline around your actual capability. If they need 8 minutes of suppression and you have 5 minutes of rounds, the Commander needs to know before the assault begins — not during it.

## AG MECHANICS

The AG's job is to ensure the gunner never runs dry.

### Ammunition Monitoring

- Interact on the gunner's weapon to check ammo status (varies by server mod stack — check for ammo icon in UI)
- Monitor continuously during sustained fire — do not wait until the rate of fire audibly changes

### Belt Linking — The 25% Rule

**Link a fresh belt when ammo drops to approximately 25% remaining.**

Do not wait until the belt runs empty. An empty gun in an active fire mission is a critical failure — the gun goes silent exactly when the assault element is counting on you. At 25% remaining, the gunner still has rounds. Link the fresh belt now so the transition is seamless and uninterrupted.

### Belt Distribution — The Backpack System

**The gunner carries most belts in their backpack, not in their vest/pouches.**

This is intentional. The gunner's backpack is the team's ammunition reserve. The AG's responsibilities include:
- Accessing the gunner's backpack to pull belts as needed
- Maintaining a ready supply near the gun position
- Quick-moving to a nearby friendly teammate to take their belt and return when the team supply runs low

Track total belts in the system. When the gunner's backpack reaches 2–3 belts remaining, brief the squad leader that resupply is needed.

## TALKING GUNS — TWO-GUN COORDINATION

When two MG teams are operating in the same sector, they coordinate using the **talking guns** technique. This doubles ammunition sustainability while maintaining continuous suppressive presence.

**Setup:**
1. Both guns initiate with a simultaneous mad minute
2. Designate **Gun One** and **Gun Two** on the radio

**Execution — alternating bursts at sustained rate:**

| Step | Gun One | Gun Two |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mad minute | Mad minute (simultaneous) |
| 2 | Fire sustained burst | Hold |
| 3 | Calls "Gun one" over radio | — |
| 4 | Hold | Fire sustained burst |
| 5 | — | Calls "Gun two" over radio |
| Repeat | ← → | alternating |

**Effect:** The enemy perceives continuous fire. From their perspective, a gun is always firing. From your perspective, each gun fires at half the rate — effectively **doubling the engagement time on the same ammunition load.**

**With three guns (larger element):** Rotate Gun One → Gun Two → Gun Three. Each gun fires once every 12 seconds at sustained cadence, extending the engagement window further while ammunition lasts.

## MG EMPLOYMENT PRINCIPLES

- **Never position the MG on the same axis as the assault element's advance.** The MG fires along the support-by-fire axis; the assault moves perpendicular or on a flanking axis to avoid fratricide.
- **Identify your final protective line before the assault begins.** Know at what point you will shift or lift fire as the assault element closes to danger close.
- **The MG is not a point weapon** — it creates a beaten zone (an area of concentrated fall of shot). Assign a DMR or rifle for specific point targets; use the MG for area suppression.
- **The MG must be behind cover, not just concealment.** The gun position will receive concentrated counter-fire as priority target. Brush and thin wood will not stop it. Concrete, earth berms, and stone walls will.
