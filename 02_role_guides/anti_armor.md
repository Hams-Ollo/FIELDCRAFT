# ANTI-ARMOR GUIDE
## Anti-Tank Rifleman (ATR) and Assistant ATR — WCS Conflict Operations

> **Doctrine basis:** WCS Infantry Handbook — Loadout Handbook
> **Applies to:** WCS NA/EU Conflict servers — ATR and Asst ATR roles
> **Position:** 1-1.7 (ATR) and 1-1.8 (Asst ATR) within the fire team

---

## ROLE PURPOSE

The Anti-Tank Rifleman is the infantry squad's primary answer to armored threats. On WCS Conflict, armor is persistent and can change the outcome of an entire objective. One AT team can destroy a vehicle that otherwise shuts down a squad's advance or pins them in position.

The ATR does not fight alone. The two-man team (ATR + Asst ATR) operates as a unit, with the assistant carrying reloads and providing security while the ATR engages.

## THE MK153 SMAW

The SMAW (**Shoulder-launched Multi-purpose Assault Weapon**) is the NATO primary AT launcher in WCS.

**Ammunition — Mk 6 Mod 0 HEAA (High Explosive Anti-Armor):**
- Effective range: approx. 500 m (optimum 200–400 m)
- Defeats light-to-medium armor (IFVs, APCs, light vehicles). Use multiple hits for MBT.
- Back-blast hazard: 20 m behind the muzzle — clear before firing

**Mounting and use:**
1. Equip the SMAW from inventory
2. Load a rocket via interaction menu
3. Aim for the target's most vulnerable aspect (rear or flank)
4. Call *"BACK BLAST CLEAR"* before firing — Asst ATR confirms the rear arc is clear
5. Fire on Asst ATR's confirmation

**The spotter scope:** The SMAW has an attached 9-power spotter scope with a ballistic reticle. Use it to estimate range and confirm target identification before engaging — reduces wasted rounds.

## TEAM TACTICS

### The Two-Man AT Team
The ATR and Asst ATR operate as a bound pair — never more than 50 m apart during engagement.

**ATR responsibilities:**
- Identifies target, selects firing position
- Evaluates back-blast clearance
- Engages on Asst ATR's "clear" call
- Relocates immediately after each shot

**Asst ATR responsibilities:**
- Carries reloads; passes them to ATR
- Clears the back-blast zone before each shot
- Provides close-in security while ATR is focused downrange through optics
- Alternate fire team lead if ATR becomes casualty

### AT Pair Movement
Move using a modified bounding technique:
- ATR bounds to firing position; Asst ATR maintains overwatch
- Asst ATR bounds up once ATR is established
- Then: ATR fires, both bound back (range) or to a new angle

After each shot, **relocate**. Arma enemy AI (and human armor crews) will scan the firing origin within 5–10 seconds of a hit. A static AT team gets killed. A mobile AT team gets kills.

## TARGET ENGAGEMENT SEQUENCE

```
1. IDENTIFY target — confirm threat (not a friendly vehicle)
2. COMMUNICATE — report to SL: "AT team up, vehicle 9 o'clock, 200 meters."
3. SELECT firing position — cover from return fire, clear back-blast arc
4. CLEAR back-blast — Asst ATR: "CLEAR!"
5. LOAD — ATR loads rocket; Asst ATR already has next round ready
6. ENGAGE — aim for rear or flank for maximum effect
7. RELOCATE — immediately after firing, do not watch the round
8. RELOAD — Asst ATR passes next rocket; load on the move
9. ASSESS — wait for visual on BDA; request additional shots if needed
```

**Aim points by vehicle:**

| Vehicle | Aim Point |
|---|---|
| MBT (Abrams, T-80) | Rear engine deck or lower hull — most penetrable aspect |
| IFV / APC (Bradley, BMP) | Flank or rear — thin armor |
| Light vehicle | Any aspect |
| Helicopter (grounded) | Engine compartment |

## VEHICLE THREAT PRIORITY

Engage in this order when multiple vehicles are present:

1. **Active armor engaging friendlies** — immediate threat
2. **Crew-served anti-armor (ATGM vehicles)** — can kill dismounts and vehicles
3. **IFVs / APCs** — carrying enemy dismounts that will compound the threat
4. **Transport vehicles** — lower priority unless delivering reinforcements

---

## AT PLACEMENT IN THE SQUAD

The fire team leader (FTL) manages the AT pair as a distinct section within the team. Typical placement:

- **Assault element (Rifleman, AR, Grenadier)** — takes contact, fixes enemy
- **AT pair (ATR + Asst ATR)** — flanks for firing angle or holds in overwatch position
- AT pair does **not** lead the assault. They move to effective range and angle, then engage.

In a squad without dedicated support element, the AT pair is the SL's reserve — not committed until armor appears. Wasting rockets on infantry is a critical error. The SL approves all AT engagements if time permits.

---

## BACKBLAST SAFETY

**Every SMAW shot has a 20-meter backblast danger area.** Before every shot:

1. ATR calls: *"Backblast area — clear!"*
2. Asst ATR physically checks the rear 20 meters and 60-degree cone behind the tube
3. Asst ATR calls: *"Clear!"*
4. ATR fires only on confirmation

**Never fire from:**
- Inside a building unless the rear has large openings to vent backblast
- Tight corridors or alleyways
- With friendly elements behind you

**Russian RPG-7V1 has similar backblast considerations** — same safety procedure applies.
