# ASSAULT RIFLEMAN ROLE GUIDE
## The Fire Team's Foundation

> **Doctrine basis:** FM 7-8 (Infantry Platoon and Squad); ATP 3-21.8 Chapter 2
> **Application:** WCS servers — the entry-level combat role and backbone of all small unit tactics

---

## THE RIFLEMAN'S ROLE

The Assault Rifleman is the building block from which all small unit tactics are constructed. Every specialized role — team leader, grenadier, machine gunner — exists in relationship to the rifleman. You are the player who closes with the enemy. You are the player who moves under fire while your buddy covers you.

The rifleman's job has three components, in this order:

1. **Maintain sector security** — cover your assigned direction at all times
2. **Support your buddy team** — when your buddy moves, you fire; when you fire, your buddy moves
3. **Execute fire and movement** — close with and destroy the enemy by fire and maneuver

You do not need to understand the whole platoon plan. You need to understand your sector, your buddy, and what you were told to do next.

---

## FIRE TEAM POSITION

The fire team wedge places the rifleman at **right-rear** — behind and to the right of the team leader.

```
         ● TL (center-front)
        / \
  AR ●     ● GRN
       \   /
        ● RFL ← YOU (right-rear)
```

**Your sector:** 45 degrees to the right. Your job is to cover the dead space that the AR (left-rear) cannot see. Every player in the wedge has a different sector — yours is right flank.

**Why right-rear:** The rifleman's sector overlaps with the AR to prevent any gap on the right side of the wedge. If an enemy works around the right flank, the rifleman detects it first.

**Important:** Your sector assignment does not change because you are curious about what's happening on the left or front. Eyes right, always.

---

## BUDDY PAIR RELATIONSHIP

Your buddy pair partner is the Grenadier (or whoever the TL assigns as your pair). You and your buddy operate as an inseparable unit.

| State | Your action | Buddy's action |
|---|---|---|
| Moving | Point weapon toward your sector, scan while moving | Covers your movement, increases fire if threat present |
| Set and covering | Fire into enemy position | Moves to next bound |
| Taking fire | Return fire immediately, call direction | Assesses and suppresses or moves |
| Buddy hit | Move to buddy, render buddy aid | N/A — you are the responder |

**The buddy pair contract:** You do not leave your buddy. If your buddy goes down, you pull them to cover before anything else. You do not continue the assault without accounting for your buddy. You do not break contact without knowing where your buddy is.

## WEAPON EMPLOYMENT

### Fire Discipline

The rifleman does not fire until there is an identified target or an order to suppress. Random fire gives your position away, wastes ammunition, and degrades fire mission coordination. Fire on command or on positive contact only.

**Exception:** If you take direct fire (rounds passing near you, impacts on your cover), return fire immediately in the direction of contact. This is not a rules violation — it is self-preservation and it buys the SL the time to assess.

### Fire Modes in WCS

| Mode | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Semi-automatic** | Default. Accurate, ammunition-efficient. Use at all ranges against identified targets. |
| **Burst (3-round)** | Final assault at close range (under 50 meters). Higher hit density on a closing target. |
| **Automatic** | Only if assaulting through at close range with an automatic-capable weapon. Never for area suppression — that task belongs to the AR. |

**The rifleman is NOT the suppression weapon.** The AR suppresses. You provide aimed fire at visible enemies and participate in fire and movement. If there is no identified target, you do not spray ammunition at a treeline.

### Trigger Discipline in WCS

- Confirm your target before firing — friendly casualties happen when players fire at movement instead of confirmed threats
- Use LRF or a scope to confirm identity at 200+ meters
- If unsure: do not fire — call it on radio: *"Movement, 200 meters, 2 o'clock, unconfirmed"*

## FIRE AND MOVEMENT — YOUR EXECUTION

The fire team executes fire and movement as buddy pairs. As the rifleman, you bound with (or cover) the Grenadier.

**When your buddy calls "Moving!":**
1. Increase your rate of fire — you are covering a moving friendly
2. Keep fire on the enemy position — volume of fire matters when someone is running in the open
3. Watch for the "Set!" call — when it comes, your buddy is in cover and covering you
4. Call "Moving!" and bound to your next position

**When you call "Moving!":**
1. Identify your next bound — a specific piece of cover, 10–20 meters away maximum
2. Call "Moving!" — wait for "Move!" from your buddy
3. Sprint directly to your target cover — no hesitation, no stopping halfway
4. Get behind cover, orient toward the enemy, call "Set!"

**Never bound blind.** Always know exactly where you are going before you stand up. Two seconds to identify cover is faster and safer than stopping in the open.

---

## ACTIONS ON CONTACT

When the element takes fire, your actions are automatic — no orders needed.

**Step 1:** Return fire in the direction of contact — volume first, accuracy second. The goal is to immediately reduce the enemy's accuracy so the SL can assess.

**Step 2:** Drop to prone or get behind cover — movement and low profile make you a harder target.

**Step 3:** Report if you have information — direction, range, weapon type. One short transmission only.

**Step 4:** Listen for the SL's order. The SL will assign you to the assault or SBF element. Do not move until told.

**If no orders come within 10 seconds:** Continue suppression and hold cover. The SL may be managing the radio or assessing. Hold your position and keep firing until someone with authority gives direction.

---

## SUPPORT BY FIRE (SBF)

When assigned to the SBF element, your job is to prevent the enemy from shooting at the assault element while they maneuver.

**SBF execution:**
1. Establish a firing position with line of sight to the enemy
2. Fire at enemy positions — visible enemies and weapon positions, not randomly into cover
3. Maintain sustained fire presence — not every second, but consistent enough to keep the enemy's heads down
4. Listen for the assault element's "Assaulting!" call
5. Increase to rapid fire when the assault element is within 100 meters
6. **Shift or cease fire on the SL's call when the assault element reaches 35 meters**

> **Critical:** You must track the assault element's position at all times. Shifting fire too early leaves them unsuppressed. Continuing to fire too late kills friendly players. The "Assaulting!" call is your warning — anticipate the shift.

---

## THE ASSAULT ELEMENT

When assigned to assault, your job is to close with the enemy while the SBF element suppresses.

**Assault execution:**
1. Bound forward using fire and movement with your buddy pair
2. When within 50 meters: transition to rapid fire and continuous forward movement
3. When within 25 meters: assault through the position in a line
4. Clear each subposition — call "Clear!" for each one
5. Consolidate on the far side facing outward to cover counterattack routes

**In the assault, you do not stop.** Fire and move, fire and move, until you are through the position. Players who stop to take cover during the final assault stall the element and get caught in the danger zone.

---

## CONSOLIDATION AND SEARCH

After the position is taken:

**Face out immediately.** Do not look at the cleared position — cover the flanks to prevent counterattack. The SL designates searchers.

**If designated to search** (see [Ambush SOP](../03_tactical_sop/ambush_sop.md) for full two-man search technique):
- One guard at the head of the body, weapon oriented toward threat direction
- One searcher works head to feet
- Items of intelligence value: maps, documents, radio equipment, SOI cards, rank insignia

Do not take personal items — this is intelligence collection, not looting.

---

## COMMUNICATION

The rifleman talks as little as possible on the radio. Your transmissions are for:
- **Contact report** — first sight of enemy: direction and range
- **Status update** — "Set," "Moving," or "Hit — buddy aid needed"
- **Nothing else** unless directly addressed by the SL

Do not transmit your thought process. Do not ask tactical questions during a firefight. Do not report what you can see if it is not relevant to the squad's immediate situation. **Brief, direct, and only when necessary.**

---

## PRIORITY OF WORK (HALTS AND DEFENSIVE POSITIONS)

Whenever the element stops:
1. **Establish security** — orient toward your assigned sector immediately
2. **Report ready** — "Set" on radio when your position covers your sector
3. **Improve your position** — get behind cover, dig or pile if time allows
4. **Wait for orders** — do not redistribute ammunition, eat, or relax until the SL clears it

The rifleman who immediately faces his sector and goes prone before asking what's happening is the rifleman who keeps the squad alive.
