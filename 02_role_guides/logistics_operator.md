# LOGISTICS OPERATOR ROLE GUIDE
## Supply Chain Management for WCS Conflict

> **Application:** WCS servers — supply economy, truck operations, AI driver management
> **Source:** Ironbeard WCS Logistics Guide

---

## WHY LOGISTICS WINS MATCHES

WCS Conflict is an economy war. Every spawn costs supplies. Every vehicle, every arsenal item, every fortification, every repair costs supplies. The team that controls supply flow controls what the enemy can and cannot do.

A single logistics operator running consistent supply circuits does more to enable team combat power than almost any other role. A team running dry in the middle of a push cannot spawn vehicles, cannot build FOBs, and cannot reinforce the front.

You are the sustaining force behind every assault.

---

## WHAT SUPPLIES UNLOCK

Understanding what supplies fund is essential to prioritizing your runs.

| Supply Level | What It Enables |
|---|---|
| Base supplies | Spawn points outside MOB |
| Higher supply | Vehicles from vehicle depots |
| Sustained supply | Arsenal (weapons and gear loadouts) |
| High supply | Construction (FOBs, fortifications) |
| Large reserves | Vehicle repairs |

When a frontline FOB goes dry, players cannot spawn there. When a main base goes dry, the full arsenal becomes inaccessible. Keep the supply chain running — it is never optional.

---

## SUPPLY SOURCES

**Green map icons — Depots (primary source):** Clearly marked on the map. Go here for large, reliable supply hauls. Will eventually deplete; track what you have already pulled.

**Hidden caches — Unmarked:** Nearly every town has at least one supply cache not shown on the map. These are not marked. Learning the common locations on your server's maps makes you significantly more effective than a logistics operator who only uses marked depots.

**Friendly deep-territory bases:** If forward depots are depleted or contested, fall back to the main base or rear-area installations for supply.

---

## VEHICLE SELECTION

**Transport Truck — Best option for supply running.** Highest supply capacity. Purpose-built for the role. If a transport truck is available at the pool, take it.

**Helicopter — Large load/unload radius.** Helicopters can load and unload supplies from a significantly larger area than ground vehicles. Useful for resupplying forward positions that trucks cannot safely reach. Requires pilot proficiency — do not take the helicopter if you are not comfortable landing it near a contested FOB.

**Light vehicles — Last resort.** Low capacity. Use only when no truck is available and the team needs supply urgently.

---

## LOADING SUPPLIES

**Standard method:**
- Drive to a supply source (depot or cache)
- Approach the truck bed within 10 meters
- Open the **Action Menu** (hold action key) on the truck bed
- Select "Load Supplies"

**Alternate method if action menu is unresponsive:**
- Open the inventory screen at a signpost near the supply source
- Transfer supplies manually through the inventory interface

> **Know the service detection range.** The truck must be within the correct radius for the action to appear. If nothing shows in the action menu, reposition 2–3 meters closer to the supply source.

---

## UNLOADING SUPPLIES

- Drive to the destination FOB or base
- Park within service detection range of the base's supply receiving point
- Use the Action Menu on the truck bed → select "Unload Supplies"
- Position the truck near the **canopy or signpost side** of the receiving structure for the cleanest detection response

> **XP CRITICAL:** You must be the player performing the unload action to receive XP credit. If a teammate unloads your truck while you are away from it, **you receive zero XP** for that run. Stay with your truck through the unload.

---

## DESYNC FIX

If the truck appears loaded but supply transfer is not registering on unload:

1. Put the truck in **reverse** briefly
2. Drive **forward** again
3. Attempt the unload action

This breaks the desync glitch that occasionally prevents supply transfer from completing.

## SOLO LOGISTICS KIT

When running supply solo with no squad support:

**Recommended loadout:**
- Light weapon (minimal weight — you are driving, not fighting)
- **Radio backpack** (critical — deploy it near your supply source when working a circuit)

The radio backpack extends radio coverage into areas you are working. Position it strategically when running solo supply circuits — it doubles as a relay point and keeps you on the platoon net while operating in rear areas.

---

## AI LOGISTICS DRIVERS

When a Commander has assigned AI logistics teams, ground operators can direct them:

**Setup:**
1. Commander selects the AI logistics team from the command interface
2. Position the AI near the transport truck
3. Issue a **Move order** from the map to the target destination

**Range requirement:** AI must be positioned within supply-loading range of the source before pickup. Escort them into position if needed.

**Note:** AI drivers are effective for steady-state circuits on established routes. Use human operators for critical frontline resupply runs — AI teams do not adapt to contact, route changes, or FOB status changes.

---

## RADIO RELAY AND ANTENNA PLACEMENT

When building or repositioning radio relay antennas during a logistics run:

- **Face the antenna opening toward the objective or cap zone** — this maximizes effective range in the direction that matters operationally
- **Build living quarters near supply points when possible** — this reduces loadout cost for players spawning at or near that position, freeing supply budget for other uses

Antenna placement is a force multiplier. A single well-placed relay antenna can extend coverage to positions that would otherwise be unreachable for fire missions and coordination.

---

## SUPPLY PRIORITIES

**When a Commander is active**, they will assign supply priority 1/2/3 to bases and a maximum supply cap. As logistics operator:
- Deliver to Priority 1 first, always
- Do not overfill past the commander's set cap — excess supply is wasted capacity
- Communicate to the Commander when a depot source is depleted so they can update priorities

**When no Commander is active**, use your own assessment:
1. Frontline FOBs running dry = highest urgency (squad cannot spawn)
2. Main Base arsenal at risk = high urgency (everyone loses loadout access)
3. Mid-chain FOBs = steady-state priority

---

## THE LOGISTICS LOOP

```
MOB → Load at Depot/Cache → Drive to Priority FOB → Unload (stay for XP) → Return → Repeat
```

**Build the relay chain:** MOB → mid-FOB → frontline FOB. Shorter legs mean faster resupply cycles and more supply delivered per hour than a single long run from MOB to front.
