## PART 1: What Is Arma Reforger & WCS? {#part-1}

### The Game

Arma Reforger is a hardcore military simulation by Bohemia Interactive — available on PC, Xbox, and PS5. Unlike mainstream shooters, it rewards teamwork, communication, and tactical thinking over twitch reflexes. Large maps, realistic ballistics, a full medical system, base-building, and combined-arms logistics make every match feel like an actual operation.

### The WCS Community

Worst Case Scenario (WCS) is a modding community founded in April 2024 focused on hyper-realistic content for Arma Reforger. Their custom mods — **WCS Core**, **WCS Arsenal**, and **WCS Armaments** — layer authentic gear, ballistic values, and vehicles on top of the base game, creating one of the most immersive PvP experiences on any platform.

WCS runs **16+ servers globally** across multiple maps. All servers are free to play — you just need a legal copy of Arma Reforger and the WCS mods installed.

## PART 2: Getting Started — Before You Join {#part-2}

### Step 1: Play the In-Game Tutorial FIRST

Do **not** skip this. From the main menu go to **Scenarios > Training**. It covers movement, weapons handling, the medical system, navigation, vehicles, helicopters, and the Conflict game mode. The training is modular — you can replay any section.

> ⚠️ **MANDATORY:** If you join a WCS server without completing the tutorial, you will be lost and a liability to your squad. The tutorial takes ~45 minutes and will save hours of frustration.

### Step 2: Install WCS Mods

WCS servers require their custom mods. Here is how to get them:

- Launch Arma Reforger and go to **Workshop**
- Search for: **WCS Core**, **WCS Arsenal**, **WCS Armaments**
- Subscribe and download all three
- The game will also prompt you to download mods automatically when you first join a WCS server

> 💡 **PRO TIP:** If you experience crash-on-spawn, make sure all mods are fully downloaded and up to date. Partial mod downloads are the #1 cause of spawn crashes on WCS servers.

### Step 3: Join a WCS Server

- Go to **Multiplayer > Favorite** and star the WCS servers (look for `[WCS]` prefix and `discord.armawcs.com` in the name)
- **NA players:** NA1, NA2, NA3 Serhiivka and Glenwood servers — best ping (~44ms)
- **EU players:** EU2–EU6 available at ~120ms ping
- Servers run **128 players max** — if full, wait for a slot or try another map

## PART 3: The Conflict Game Mode — How to Win {#part-3}

### The Core Objective

Conflict is WCS's primary game mode. Two factions — **NATO** (Blue) and **Russia** (Red) — fight for control of strategic locations across the map. To **WIN**, your team must capture and **HOLD** a certain number of key locations shown in the upper-right HUD. These locations must be within your faction's **radio signal range** to count.

Think of it as a living, breathing tug-of-war across kilometers of terrain — where logistics, radio coverage, and unit cohesion matter as much as gunfighting.

### Map Objective Color Key

| Color | Type | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| 🟣 **MAGENTA/PURPLE** | Primary objective | **WIN/LOSE — Always prioritize** |
| ⚫ **BLACK** | Secondary objective | Important for supply/radio chain |
| 🔵 **BLUE** (your color) | Friendly-held | Defend and supply |
| 🔴 **RED** (enemy color) | Enemy-held | Attack and capture |

> **Red is always Russia. Blue is always NATO on the map.**

### The Radio Signal — The Invisible Win Condition

You **CANNOT** capture a base unless it is within your faction's radio range. Radio range starts from your Main Operating Base (MOB) and extends by:

- Capturing communication relay towers and radio stations
- Deploying **Mobile Command Unit (MCU)** trucks that extend the signal
- Building radio relay stations at captured FOBs

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Many new players waste time pushing deep into enemy territory only to find they can't cap the base. Always check the map — if the objective doesn't have a connecting line to your radio network, get a radio relay there **FIRST**.

### Objective Priority Matrix

| Priority | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — CRITICAL** | Extend radio coverage | Can't cap anything without it |
| **2 — HIGH** | Secure supply depots | Feeds FOB construction and arsenals |
| **3 — HIGH** | Build and defend FOBs | Provides frontline spawn and resupply |
| **4 — MEDIUM** | Cap purple (primary) objectives | These directly win matches |
| **5 — LOWER** | Hold and fortify captured ground | Prevents enemy recapture |

> 💡 **VOLUNTEER BONUS:** Open the map and click an objective before capping it to register as a volunteer. You earn significantly more XP from the capture even if you arrive late — great for leveling up fast.

## PART 4: Logistics — The Lifeblood {#part-4}

### Why Logistics Matters More Than Gunfighting

The team with better logistics wins — period. Supplies are required for everything: spawning, building structures, equipping loadouts, and fielding vehicles. A squad that ignores logistics will eventually be unable to spawn at the front, ceding the map by default.

### The Supply Chain — How It Works

- **Main Operating Base (MOB):** Your team's HQ. Supplies regenerate here slowly over time
- **Supply Depots:** Neutral/capturable locations with large stockpiles — your main fill points
- **FOBs (Forward Operating Bases):** Player-built forward positions — supplies must be trucked here
- **Construction Trucks:** Load supplies from a depot, drive to FOB, unload. Repeat. This is the core logistics loop

### The Logistics Loop — Step by Step

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| **1** | Go to a vehicle maintenance point at your MOB. Request a logistics truck |
| **2** | Drive to a supply depot. Use the action menu (scroll wheel) to access the Construction Interface |
| **3** | Load supplies into the truck. Fill to capacity |
| **4** | Drive to the FOB or base that needs supplies |
| **5** | Unload using the same Construction Interface |
| **6** | Repeat. Build a relay: **MOB > mid-point FOB > frontline FOB** to cut round-trip time |

> 💡 **PRO TIP:** Helicopters can outperform trucks for logistics when roads are contested. A skilled pilot running supply runs between safe LZs can sustain a frontline FOB faster than a truck navigating a dangerous route.

> ⚠️ **SERVER RULE:** Only load/unload supplies from your OWN vehicles. Do NOT dump supplies on the ground to drain supply points — this results in a ban.

## PART 5: Base Building — Establishing the FOB {#part-5}

### What Is a FOB and Why Does It Matter?

A Forward Operating Base provides a spawn point closer to the action, an arsenal to rearm, and defensive structures to hold the line. Without FOBs, your squad spawns at the MOB and runs 2km to the front — losing time and momentum every death.

### Construction Vehicle Types

| Vehicle | Role |
|---|---|
| **Construction Truck** | Establishes new FOBs, builds support structures and fortifications |
| **Command Truck / MCU** | Mobile spawn point AND extends radio range |
| **Medical Vehicle** | Transports casualties and provides field hospital function |
| **Logistics Truck** | Maximum supply capacity — the workhorse of the supply chain |

### FOB Building Priority Order

| Order | Structure | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **1st** | Radio Relay Station | Without this, the FOB may not be in radio network range |
| **2nd** | Small Vehicle Depot | Allows spawning of light vehicles for patrol and movement |
| **3rd** | AI Tent | Spawns defensive AI. Reduces respawn costs |
| **4th** | Arsenal | Allows full custom loadouts at the front |
| **5th** | Defensive Fortifications | Sandbags, barbed wire, dragon's teeth — harden the perimeter |
| **6th** | Field Hospital | Full injury recovery once the FOB is established and contested |

> 💡 **PRO TIP:** Use the shovel (Entrenching Tool) to dig up trees and bushes around FOBs. Clears enemy concealment along approach routes and improves defensive sightlines.

## PART 6: Loadouts & The WCS Arsenal {#part-6}

### The Weight System

Every item has a weight in kilograms. Your total carry weight directly affects combat performance:

- **Over-encumbered:** You cannot sprint. Cannot hold sights steady. You are a walking target
- Heavy plate carriers with full protection stop more rounds but drain stamina quickly
- Light chest rigs allow faster movement — better for recon and flanking, less protection
- Your weapon's weight stacks with your kit — heavy launchers require you to strip weight elsewhere

> ⚠️ **WEIGHT WARNING:** Aim to stay **below 40kg** for regular infantry. Recon targets 30–35kg. Going over 45kg makes you a liability.

### Mandatory IFAK — Non-Negotiable

Medics use **your** supplies first before pulling from their own pack. Every soldier carries a baseline IFAK. This is SOP — no exceptions.

| Item | Quantity | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Tourniquet | 4x | Stop massive limb bleeding |
| Bandage | 4x | Pack wounds, stop minor bleeding |
| Epinephrine Auto-injector | 4x | Revive unconscious teammates |
| Morphine Auto-injector | 1x | Pain management |
| Saline Bag (500ml) | 1x | Replace lost blood volume |

### Standard Gear Checklist — All Roles

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Map + Compass + Watch | Navigation essentials |
| **Laser Range Finder** | **MANDATORY** — call accurate distances to SL and support |
| Short-Range Radio | Your squad net radio. Always on |
| **NVGs + IR Laser** | **MANDATORY** — WCS servers run a day/night cycle |
| Entrenching Tool (Shovel) | Build FOBs, clear sightlines, destroy cover |
| Wire Cutters | Breach concertina wire and obstacles |
| 7x Primary Magazines | Baseline ammo load |
| 2x Frag Grenades | Minimum. Always call "Frag out!" before throwing |
| 2x White Smoke | Concealment during movement and casualty care |
| 1x Green Smoke | Signal friendly helicopters and EVAC |

## PART 7: Role Guides — Find Your Fit {#part-7}

### 1. Assault Rifleman — Tip of the Spear

The backbone of any squad. Clearing buildings, pushing objectives, and keeping the enemy pinned while support elements work.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Weapon** | M4A1 or AK-74M with red dot, holographic, or 1-4x LPVO |
| **Key Gear** | Standard plate carrier. 7+ mags. 1x AT4/LAW for vehicles. C4 + detonator (at least 1-2 members per fireteam) |
| **Tactics** | Push under MG suppression. Check corners. Announce frags. Move fast, communicate faster |

---

### 2. Machine Gunner / Assistant Gunner — Base of Fire

**This is a TWO-PERSON job.** An MG without an AG runs dry and dies. The MG does NOT clear buildings — you set up 100–200m back at elevation and suppress so assault can advance.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Gunner Weapon** | M240B, PKM, or M249 SAW. 3–4 box magazines. Lighter armor to offset weapon weight |
| **AG Weapon** | Standard assault rifle. Backpack loaded entirely with extra ammo boxes for the gunner |
| **Tactics** | AG spots targets and calls range corrections using LRF. Gunner conserves ammo between bursts |

---

### 3. Anti-Armor / Anti-Air — The Heavy Hitters

Your job is killing vehicles, not infantry. Stay out of the meatgrinder. Find high ground overlooking supply routes and ambush enemy armor columns.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Weapon** | M3 MAAWS Carl Gustaf, Javelin, RPG-7V2 for armor; Stinger/Igla for air threats |
| **Sidearm** | Lightweight carbine or SMG only — rockets are extremely heavy |
| **Tactics** | Always work in pairs — one rocket system, one ammo bearer. Stay in overwatch positions on high ground |

---

### 4. Combat Medic — The Hardcore Healer

Your weapon is your radio and medical bag. You are **not** a shooter — you are a combat multiplier. Every player you keep alive is one more gun on the line.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Weapon** | Lightweight carbine, no heavy optics |
| **Medical Pack** | 8x Bandages, 6–8x Tourniquets, 4x Saline, 5x Epi, 5x Morphine, Splints |
| **Tactics** | Never lead the stack. Stay center or rear. When someone goes down: 1) call for covering fire, 2) drag to hard cover, 3) THEN treat. Never treat in the open |

> ⚠️ **REMINDER:** Tourniquet first → Bandage → Saline → Epinephrine → Morphine. Always in that order.

---

### 5. Reconnaissance — The Infiltrators

Your primary weapon is the radio and your Laser Range Finder. Shoot only if compromised. A recon team that dies gives the enemy intel — a recon team that lives gives us fire mission coordinates.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Weapon** | Suppressed DMR (M110, SVD) or suppressed carbine. High-power variable optic (6–24x) |
| **Kit** | Light chest rig only — ditch heavy plates. Long-range backpack radio for Platoon Net |
| **Tactics** | Concealed position overlooking enemy base. Use LRF and radio to feed grid coordinates. Call for fire rather than engaging directly |

---

### 6. Armor & Mechanized — The Cavalry

**A tank without infantry support is a coffin.** Coordinate with the Squad Leader before pushing into any urban area. Commander watches for enemy AT infantry at all times.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Weapon** | AKS-74U or M4 CQBR — compact for vehicle interiors |
| **Key Gear** | **Repair Tool — MANDATORY for ALL crew.** Fix mobility kills and engine damage in the field |
| **Tactics** | Never solo push. Always infantry bounding alongside. Commander observes from hatch and radios contacts to Platoon Net |

---

### 7. Logistics Operator — The Unsung Hero

This role wins matches. While others fight, logistics operators build the infrastructure that keeps the entire team alive and armed.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vehicle** | Large logistics truck or helicopter for speed runs |
| **Kit** | Standard loadout — you'll spend most time driving, not fighting |
| **Tactics** | Establish a relay: MOB > mid-point FOB > frontline FOB. Coordinate with builders to know what structures need supplies next |

## PART 8: Communications SOP {#part-8}

> ⚠️ **SERVER RULE:** Use English in ALL platoon voice channels. No music or spam. Squad Leaders, Pilots, and Armor Commanders **MUST** have active microphones.

### Radio Net Structure

| Net | Who Uses It / Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Platoon Net (Long Range)** | Squad Leaders, Pilots, Armor Commanders ONLY. Macro strategy, calling transport, coordinating multi-squad pushes |
| **Squad Net (Short Range)** | Your squad. Combat chatter and immediate firefight coordination. Keep it relevant |
| **Proximity Chat** | Anyone nearby — friend or foe can hear. Local coordination only |

### Brevity Codes

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Contact [Direction + Distance]` | Enemy spotted and engaging. E.g. "Contact North, 200 meters!" |
| `TIC` | Troops in Contact — we are taking effective fire and pinned |
| `Winchester` | Out of ammunition |
| `RTB` | Returning to Base — critical for pilots and logistics |
| `Danger Close` | Friendly troops within 100m of called fire mission |
| `Frag Out` | Announce BEFORE throwing any grenade |
| `Litter` | We have a casualty requiring immediate medical attention |
| `Check Fire` | STOP FIRING — friendly troops or friendly fire risk |

### Group Setup

- Select **NATO or Russia** at faction selection — you **CANNOT** switch mid-match
- Join an existing group matching your role: Assault, Recon, Anti-Tank, Engineer, Transport
- To create a new group: you need sufficient rank AND the previous group of the same type must be more than half full
- Name your squad clearly (e.g. *"Blue Falcon — forward recon and overwatch support"*)

## PART 9: Combat Fundamentals — Fighting Smart {#part-9}

### Movement and Spacing

**Spacing saves lives.** Maintain **10–15 meters between players** at all times. Cluster together and one grenade kills your entire squad.

| Formation | Use Case |
|---|---|
| **The Wedge** | Standard patrol. Point man leads, others fan out left and right rear. 270° coverage |
| **Column** | Road movement or dense terrain where spacing is forced |
| **Bounding Overwatch** | Crossing open ground. Element A covers; Element B sprints to next cover. Alternate |
| **On-Line** | Final assault on a position — all elements advance simultaneously |

### React to Contact — The 3 Ds

The moment you take fire, execute immediately:

1. **DISTANCE** — Return fire even if you can't see the enemy. Volume of fire buys time and space
2. **DIRECTION** — Identify and call the direction: *"Contact East! 150 meters!"*
3. **DESCRIPTION** — Describe what you see: *"Squad of infantry, treeline at the road junction"*

After the initial reaction: seek hard cover, get eyes on the enemy, and transition to deliberate suppression and assault.

### Support By Fire (SBF) — The Core Tactic

- **Element A (Support):** Establishes heavy suppressive fire on the enemy position — pinning them behind cover
- **Element B (Assault):** Uses the suppression to flank — moving fast through dead ground to hit from the side
- Element A **lifts or shifts** fire as Element B closes to avoid fratricide. Communication is critical

### The L-Shape Ambush — Hunting Supply Trucks

WCS supply trucks are high-value targets. The L-shape ambush is ideal for road interdiction:

- **Element A** sets up along the road (long leg of the L) — the primary kill element
- **Element B** sets up perpendicular at the far end of the kill zone (short leg) — the cut-off element
- When the truck enters the kill zone, **all elements fire simultaneously**
- AT elements target the engine; rifles target crew if they dismount

> 💡 **PRO TIP:** Use C4 and AT mines to destroy enemy roadblocks and dragon's teeth. AT mines buried on key crossroads are invisible to drivers and can deny entire supply corridors.

## PART 10: Map-Specific Strategies {#part-10}

### WCS Map Overview

| Map | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|
| **Glenwood (East/West)** | River valley divides the map. Radio control of bridges is critical. Yellow roads are ambush corridors. Pennsylvania and Washington town complexes are central objectives |
| **New Everon (N/S and AAS)** | Largest and most complex. Dense central town flanked by open farmland. N/S axis creates a long frontline. AAS mode uses alternating attack/defense — coordinate team-wide pushes |
| **Serhiivka (East/West)** | Industrial map with factory complexes and urban blocks. CQB-heavy in town center. Flanking through industrial zones is key. Armor effective in open eastern/western areas but vulnerable in-town |
| **Rahhaliyah (NE/SE)** | Desert/Middle Eastern terrain. Open sightlines favor long-range weapons and vehicles. Recon on high ground observes large swaths of territory. Vehicle ambushes on main highways are highly effective |
| **Chernarus (NE/SW)** | Classic Arma terrain — forested hills and valleys with central town. Radio relay stations in the hills extend coverage dramatically |

### Universal Map Principles

- Control **high ground first** — observation, LRF range, and suppression advantage
- Secure **supply depots early** — don't let the enemy establish an uncontested supply chain
- **Radio coverage** is the invisible battlefield — never push beyond your network without a mobile command unit
- Watch **objective connection chains** — prioritize objectives that unlock paths toward purple (primary) objectives
- **Identify enemy logistics patterns** — observe truck movement and interdict supply routes

## PART 11: Call for Fire — Artillery and CAS {#part-11}

### When to Call For Fire

Recon elements and forward observers do NOT attack high-value targets directly. When you spot an enemy FOB, armor column, or fortified position, you **call for player-controlled mortars or CAS**. One accurate fire mission is worth more than a squad-level assault.

### The WCS Call For Fire Format

| Step | What to Say / Do |
|---|---|
| **1. ID & Warning** | *"Command, this is Recon-1, request Fire Mission, over."* — Wait for acknowledgment |
| **2. Location** | Grid coordinate (6 or 8 digit): *"Grid 084 125."* Or landmark: *"100 meters East of the radio tower"* |
| **3. Description** | *"One logistics truck and a squad of infantry digging a FOB"* |
| **4. Method** | *"Requesting HE, three rounds, fire for effect."* Or *"Requesting CAS, one gun run, East to West"* |
| **5. Adjust Fire** | Watch impact: *"Impact seen. Add 50, Left 20."* (50m further, 20m left) |
| **6. End of Mission** | *"Target destroyed. End of mission."* |

> ⚠️ **DANGER CLOSE:** If friendly troops are within 100 meters of the target, you **MUST** announce "DANGER CLOSE" before calling for fire.

## PART 12: WCS Server Rules — Don't Get Banned {#part-12}

WCS operates a **shared ban system across all 16+ servers**. A ban on one server is a ban on all WCS servers.

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| **No Enemy Gear** | DO NOT wear enemy helmets, jackets, vests, uniforms, gloves, boots, or backpacks. Stealing enemy weapons is fine. Wearing their armor causes Blue on Blue and earns a ban |
| **Zero Tolerance Language** | Racist, homophobic, sexist, hateful, or offensive comments are a permanent ban. No exceptions |
| **Main Base Boundaries** | Stay within 15 meters of the last capture point. Do NOT fire into the enemy Main Base |
| **English in Platoon Voice** | All platoon voice must be in English. Squad voice is flexible |
| **No Helicopter Ramming** | Do not intentionally crash helicopters into vehicles or use them as weapons |
| **No Supply Dumping** | Do not dump supplies on the ground to drain depots. Bannable offense |

| **No Exploits** | Using or sharing exploit information results in a **PERMANENT** ban |

## PART 13: Rank Progression {#part-13}

### Why Rank Matters

Rank on WCS servers unlocks access to better gear, vehicles, and the ability to create new squad types. Higher-rank players can also open new squads when the previous one fills.

### Best Methods for Gaining XP

- **Volunteer Bonus:** Register on an objective before capture — earn bonus XP even if you arrive late
- **Capture Purple Objectives:** Primary objectives give significantly more XP than secondary ones
- **Medic Play:** Healing and reviving teammates generates steady XP without needing kills
- **Logistics Runs:** Supply deliveries reward logistics XP
- **Stay Alive:** Passive XP accrues over time when alive — dying frequently bleeds your rate
- **Build Structures:** FOB construction rewards construction XP
