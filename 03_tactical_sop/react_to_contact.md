# REACT TO CONTACT SOP
## Battle Drill 1 — Small Unit Response to Effective Enemy Fire

> **Doctrine basis:** FM 7-8 Chapter 5 — Battle Drills; ATP 3-21.8
> **Application:** WCS servers — squad and fire team response to unexpected enemy fire

---

## PURPOSE

React to Contact is the most important battle drill in WCS. Every squad engagement either begins with controlled contact or with an ambush you didn't see coming. How the element responds in the first five seconds determines whether the engagement becomes a coordinated firefight or a chaotic killbox.

**This SOP covers:**
- Immediate actions (0–5 seconds)
- Developing the situation (5–30 seconds)
- Maneuver to destroy (30 seconds → mission complete)
- Break contact (when and how)
- Special cases: ambush, vehicle contact, isolated element

---

## THE 3 Ds — CONTACT DOCTRINE

All contact responses follow three sequential phases. These are not simultaneous — each phase must be established before moving to the next.

### 1. DETECT
> *"You've got contact — where is it?"*

- Announce immediately on radio: **"CONTACT — [direction] — [range/landmark]"**
- Call direction as clock bearing from march direction: *"Contact, 3 o'clock, 200 meters, treeline"*
- If you cannot confirm direction: say **"Contact, unknown direction"** — do not guess

### 2. DEVELOP
> *"What are you actually dealing with?"*

- Establish a base of fire
- Identify: size, position, weapons, direction of movement
- Determine if this is an ambush (enemy has initiative) or chance contact (initiative is yours)
- Brief assessment to SL: *"Squad-sized, fortified treeline, two PKMs, no movement"*

### 3. DESTROY
> *"Now you know — maneuver and win."*

- Execute deliberate maneuver: suppress → flank → assault
- Or execute break contact if the situation is untenable

---

## IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (0–5 SECONDS)

**On taking fire, EVERY element member executes immediately — no orders required:**

1. **Return fire** — Shoot in the direction of contact. Do not wait for orders. Volume of fire buys time.
2. **Get down** — Drop to prone or seek cover. Standing under fire is fatal.
3. **Report** — Whoever has a visual calls direction and range. One person talks; everyone listens.
4. **Spread out** — Move away from adjacent players if clustered. Maintain 10-meter minimum spacing.

**The squad leader's immediate action:**
- Does NOT fire in the first seconds unless personally threatened
- Assesses: Where is the fire coming from? How many? What weapons?
- Gets on radio: **"CONTACT — [grid or landmark] — [direction] — [enemy description]"**
- Issues suppress order: *"Alpha team, suppress, 2 o'clock, 150 meters. Bravo team, bound left."*

> **Do not freeze. Return fire immediately. The enemy suppresses you to prevent you from shooting back — shooting back prevents suppression from working.**

---

## HALT PROCEDURES

When the element halts under contact:

**Short halt (under 30 seconds):**
- Drop to one knee or prone
- Cover your assigned sector — do not look at the squad leader
- No movement, no talking, no radio unless you have something to report

**Longer halt / establishing position:**
- Go prone behind cover
- Maintain assigned sectors — the element forms an improvised perimeter
- Squad leader moves to see the situation without exposing the squad to additional fire

---

## DEVELOPING THE SITUATION

After the immediate action, the squad leader has approximately 20–30 seconds to assess before the engagement locks into a pattern.

**The hasty attack sequence (FM 7-8):**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Deploy — move from march formation to tactical formation (online or wedge) |
| 2 | Establish base of fire — one element suppresses while the other maneuvers |
| 3 | Find the weakness — flank, gap in coverage, dead ground |
| 4 | Suppress the weakness — shift fire onto the flanking route |
| 5 | Attack through the weakness — assault element closes to 50 meters, then assaults |
| 6 | Consolidate and report |

**Fire team assignment:**
- **Assault element** — 3–4 players who will close with and destroy the enemy
- **Support by fire (SBF) element** — remaining players who suppress the enemy position while assault element maneuvers

The SBF element does not move until the assault element has reached the final assault line. The assault element does not move until fire superiority is established.

---

## FIRE AND MOVEMENT

Fire and movement is the mechanism that gets the assault element from cover to the enemy position. It is **not optional** and it does not stop until the assault element is through the objective.

**Execution — buddy pairs:**

1. **Partner A** aims and fires (rounds downrange suppressing the enemy position)
2. **Partner A calls:** *"Moving!"*
3. **Partner B responds:** *"Move!"* — and increases fire rate
4. **Partner A bounds** to next covered position (10–20 meters maximum)
5. **Partner A calls:** *"Set!"*
6. **Partner B calls:** *"Moving!"*
7. **Partner B bounds past** Partner A to the next position

**Continue alternating until the element reaches the assault line or breaks contact.**

> **Rules:**
> - Never both moving at the same time
> - Never standing still without firing
> - Bounds are 10–20 meters maximum — long bounds take too long under fire
> - Always move to COVER, not concealment. Bushes will not stop rounds.

---

## FIRE AND MOVEMENT RADIO CALLS

Keep contact calls short. The squad cannot process long transmissions in a firefight.

| Situation | Call |
|---|---|
| First contact | *"Contact [clock] [range/landmark]"* |
| Direction confirmed | *"Direction 2 o'clock, woodline, 150 out"* |
| Base of fire set | *"Alpha, set, suppressing"* |
| Ready to bound | *"Bravo, move"* |
| Assault element set | *"Set, 30 meters, ready to assault"* |
| Position cleared | *"Position clear, consolidating"* |
| Casualties | *"One urgent, [grid], need CASEVAC"* |

---

## ASSAULT THROUGH THE POSITION

When the assault element reaches 25–50 meters from the enemy position with suppression established:

1. **SBF increases fire rate** — cyclic if MG, rapid for rifles
2. **Assault element calls "Assaulting!"** — SBF acknowledges
3. **Assault element moves to 10 meters** — continuous fire while advancing
4. **SBF shifts or lifts fire** when assault element is danger-close (within 35 meters)
5. **Assault element clears through** — position by position, trench by trench
6. **Call "Clear!"** for each cleared subposition
7. **Consolidate on the far side** — face out, cover likely counterattack routes

> **Search enemy positions.** Collect: maps, radio frequencies, documents, SOI cards, rank insignia. These are direct intelligence for future mission planning.

---

## BREAK CONTACT

Break contact when:
- The element is outnumbered and cannot achieve fire superiority
- Taking casualties faster than inflicting them
- The SL or higher calls it

**Break contact is NOT retreat under fire without control.** It is a deliberate covered withdrawal.

### The Peel Drill

1. Rearmost player calls: *"Peeling!"*
2. All other players increase fire
3. Rearmost player runs past the formation toward the front
4. New rearmost player repeats
5. Peel continues until the element has broken contact and reached the rally point

**Peel in a direction 45–90 degrees off the axis of contact** — moving directly backward gives the enemy a perfect pursuit corridor. Move laterally first, then break off.

**Rally point:** Designate before the mission. Brief all players. If separated: *"Rally at [landmark]"* on radio.

## REACT TO AMBUSH

An ambush is a pre-planned attack from concealed positions designed to destroy the element in the kill zone. Immediate action differs based on proximity.

### Near Ambush — Enemy Within 35 Meters

> The kill zone is where you die if you stay. The only way out is through.

**Immediate action:**
1. Return fire IMMEDIATELY — maximum rate, every weapon, simultaneously
2. Assault TOWARD the ambush — close with and through the ambush position
3. Do NOT attempt the peel drill — you are too close; withdrawal is fatal at this range

**Why assault into a near ambush:** The kill zone is geometrically designed to destroy you at range. Closing to the ambush ruins the enemy's engagement solution and is the last thing they expect. Surviving a near ambush requires immediate, violent forward action.

### Far Ambush — Enemy Beyond 35 Meters

**Immediate action:**
1. Suppress toward the source of fire
2. Near-side element bounds to covered ground — not toward the far-side fire
3. Assemble and assess
4. Option A: maneuver on the position (execute hasty attack sequence)
5. Option B: peel drill to disengage

> The far ambush is effectively a react-to-contact scenario. Follow the 3 Ds.

### Vehicle in the Kill Zone

1. **Vehicle occupants:** return fire immediately from the vehicle — do not dismount while fire is incoming
2. **Driver:** drive THROUGH the kill zone at maximum speed (forward or backward — what matters is exiting)
3. **Once clear of the kill zone:** dismounts exit and establish a ground element; vehicle provides suppression
4. Re-engage from outside the kill zone as a combined vehicle and dismount element

> **Do not stop a vehicle in a kill zone.** A stationary vehicle under concentrated fire is a coffin.

---

## CONSOLIDATION AND EXPLOITATION

After the position is cleared:

**Consolidate:**
- 360-degree security — all players face outward and cover assigned sectors
- Render first aid to casualties before anything else
- Account for all players — squad leader verifies headcount
- Report to higher: *"Position cleared, [number] casualties, [number] enemy killed"*

**Exploit:**
- Search bodies for intelligence (see [Ambush SOP](ambush_sop.md) — Two-Man Search Technique)
- Mark the position if it has intelligence or tactical value
- Report enemy equipment, documents, and captured radio frequencies to command

**Move quickly after consolidation.** An enemy platoon knows where their squad was. Stay long enough to treat casualties and collect intelligence, then move.
